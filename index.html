Day 1: Basic Greetings and Introductions

Objective: Learn simple greetings, introductions, and how to ask and answer "How are you?"

1. Common Greetings

  • Hello - A general greeting for anyone, any time.
  • Hi - More casual, for friends or people you know well.
  • Good morning - Used before 12 PM.
  • Good afternoon - Used from 12 PM to 5 PM.
  • Good evening - Used after 5 PM.

Practice: Try saying each greeting aloud!

2. Introducing Yourself

  • My name is [Name] - Example: "My name is Alex."
  • I am [Age] years old - Example: "I am 20 years old."
  • I am from [Country/City] - Example: "I am from Thailand."

Practice: Write down your introduction and say it aloud.

3. Asking and Answering "How are you?"

  • Question: "How are you?"
  • Responses:
    • "I am fine, thank you." / "I'm fine, thanks."
    • "I’m good." / "I’m great!"
    • "Not bad." / "I’m okay."

Practice: Pair with "Hello" and respond naturally.

Activity: Practice a mini-conversation with these sentences. Here’s an example:

  • 1. Person A: Hello!
  • 2. Person B: Hi! How are you?
  • 3. Person A: I’m fine, thank you. And you?
  • 4. Person B: I’m good, thank you!

Repeat until you feel comfortable with the basics. Tomorrow, we’ll build on these phrases!

Day 2: Introducing Others and Asking Basic Questions

Objective: Learn to introduce someone else and ask simple questions to get to know someone.

1. Introducing Another Person

  • This is [Name] - Example: "This is Sarah."
  • He/She is my [relationship] - Example: "She is my friend."
  • He/She is from [Place] - Example: "He is from India."

Practice: Think of a friend or family member and try introducing them.

2. Basic Questions for Getting to Know Someone

  • What is your name?
    • Answer: "My name is [Name]."
  • Where are you from?
    • Answer: "I am from [Country/City]."
  • How old are you?
    • Answer: "I am [Age] years old."

Practice: Imagine meeting someone new and asking these questions.

3. Simple Conversation Practice

  • Person A: Hi! What is your name?
  • Person B: My name is Alex. And you?
  • Person A: I’m Sarah. Nice to meet you!
  • Person B: Nice to meet you too! Where are you from?
  • Person A: I am from Thailand. How about you?
  • Person B: I’m from India.

Activity: Practice introducing a friend and asking someone where they are from. Try creating a few more questions and answers on your own. Tomorrow, we’ll expand with more phrases!

  • Museum

  • Supermarket
  • Post office
  • Train station / Bus stop

Practice: Think about these places and practice saying each word.

2. Asking for Directions

  • Where is the [place]? - Example: "Where is the supermarket?"

3. Giving Simple Directions

  • It’s near the [place]. - Example: "It’s near the park."

4. Simple Conversation Practice

  • Person A: Thank you!
  • Person B: You’re welcome!

Day 9: Talking About Food and Ordering in a Restaurant

Day 10: Describing Clothing and Shopping

Objective: Learn vocabulary to describe clothes and practice simple shopping conversations.

1. Common Clothing Vocabulary

2. Describing Clothing

  • Colors: red, blue, green, black, white, yellow, etc.
  • Sizes: small, medium, large, extra-large
  • Patterns: striped, checked, plain, polka dot
  • Materials: cotton, wool, leather, silk

Practice: Choose an outfit and describe it with color, size, and pattern.

3. Shopping Phrases

  • How much is this? - To ask the price of an item.
  • Do you have this in [size/color]? - Example: "Do you have this in medium?"
  • Can I try this on? - Asking to try the clothing.
  • I’ll take it. - When you decide to buy something.
  • Can I have a discount? - Asking for a lower price.

Practice: Imagine shopping for something you want and using these phrases.

4. Simple Conversation Practice

  • Customer: Excuse me, how much is this shirt?

  • Customer: Do you have it in blue?
  • Shop Assistant: Yes, we do. Would you like to try it on?
  • Customer: Yes, please. I’ll take it!

Activity: Describe an outfit you like and imagine buying it at a store. Tomorrow, we’ll talk about making plans and inviting someone to do something together!

Day 12: Talking About Past Events

Objective: Learn how to talk about things that happened in the past.

1. Using Past Tense Verbs

  • Regular verbs: Add -ed to the verb (e.g., work -> worked).
  • Irregular verbs: Some verbs change completely (e.g., go -> went, see -> saw).

Practice: List some regular and irregular verbs, and try to remember their past forms.

2. Talking About Yesterday or Last Week

  • Yesterday, I [past tense verb] - Example: "Yesterday, I studied."
  • Last week, I [past tense verb] - Example: "Last week, I went to the beach."
  • I [past tense verb] two days ago - Example: "I called my friend two days ago."

Practice: Think about what you did yesterday and describe it in a sentence.

3. Simple Conversation Practice

  • Person A: What did you do yesterday?
  • Person B: I went shopping. How about you?
  • Person A: I watched a movie with friends.

Activity: Write a few sentences about what you did last weekend or yesterday. Tomorrow, we’ll talk about future plans and using “will” and “going to” for things you plan to do!
